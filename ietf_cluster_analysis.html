D
Actor Chain Representation
0 WG anchors 6 drafts +1 Jul 15

How the chain of acting principals — who delegated to whom — is encoded inside OAuth tokens. Two competing approaches: McGuinness Actor Profile (RFC 8693 act claim extension) and mw-oauth-actor-chain (cryptographically verifiable per-hop binding). The Intent Provenance Protocol (haberkamp-ipp, new) adds intent-carrying to the chain model.

E
Token Attenuation
0 WG anchors 7 drafts +2 Jul 15

Narrowing token scope as it propagates through delegation chains — the "least privilege for agents" problem. The McGuinness Mission-Bound OAuth blog series frames this as the central unsolved problem in agent authorization. Two new additions Jul 15: a WG baseline spec for refresh token expiration and a novel user-mediated delivery primitive.

F
Pre-action Permits & Authorization Evidence
Emerging family 10 drafts +5 Jul 15

Pre-action human authorization and post-action evidence for high-risk agent actions. The EMILIA Protocol family (5 schrock-ep-* drafts, rev -06) arrived Jul 15 and is now the most coherent unanchored family in the corpus — covering receipts, evidence chains, action evidence graphs, quorum authorization, and long-term record preservation under DORA/HIPAA/SEC mandates.

H
AIP Name Collision
BLOCKING 4 drafts

Four incompatible drafts use identical or near-identical names and acronyms — "AIP" (Agent Identity Protocol) — creating a namespace collision that must be resolved before the IETF can make progress on agent identity standardization. A fourth collision (fane-opena2a-aip) arrived in Jul 2026.

WIMSE Agent Extensions
4 WG anchors 11 drafts +3 Jul 15
